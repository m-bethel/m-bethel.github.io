Our Vision

Platform Planks

01

Economy & Cost of Living

Provide working family tax breaks and reform social services. Reduce SNAP fraud through approved item lists and benefit caps. Implement maximum benefit cost limits and fraud policing across all government programs. Support hardworking families while ensuring accountability.

02

Housing Affordability

Lower interest rates and implement strict house vetting. Prohibit corporate entities like BlackRock from mass home ownership. Secure the constitutional pursuit of "life, liberty, and happiness" by making home ownership accessible to all American families.

03

Healthcare Access

Comprehensive insurance reform with no government stipends or bailouts for insurance companies. Make healthcare affordable through market-based solutions while protecting patient choice and doctor-patient relationships.

04

Immigration & Border Control

Secure the border and fully support law enforcement agencies (FBI, DEA, ICE). Protect American workers and ensure national security through strong border enforcement and immigration system reform.

05

Environmental Policy

Implement nuclear power requirements as a clean, reliable energy source. Balance environmental protection with energy independence and economic growth through innovative, practical solutions.

06

Voting Rights

Require proof of citizenship for voting, provided free of charge to all eligible citizens. Ensure election integrity while guaranteeing every American's right to participate in our democracy.

07

Tax Reduction

Eliminate "double taxation" and implement comprehensive fraud detection across all levels of government. Reduce the tax burden on working families while ensuring accountability in government spending.

08

Criminal Justice Reform

Increase judge and district attorney pay and accountability. Focus on rehabilitation and reintegration into society. Deinstitutionalize individuals who can successfully reenter communities while maintaining public safety.

09

Political Reform & Term Limits

End career politicians. Require U.S.-born citizenship for all political offices. Cap compensation at 150% of constituent average income. Ban single-issue bills to ensure comprehensive legislation and accountability.

10

International Diplomacy

Exit NATO and stop sending taxpayer money to foreign governments. Prioritize American interests and invest in our own communities rather than funding overseas conflicts and international organizations.

Fundraising Approach

We're building a grassroots-powered movement funded by the people, not special interests. Our multi-pronged fundraising strategy includes reaching out to private donors who believe in our mission, launching crowdfunding campaigns to engage supporters at every level, and selling branded merchandise that spreads our message. We're actively seeking sponsorships from organizations aligned with our values and exploring available government funding options. Every dollar raised goes directly toward amplifying the voices of hardworking Americans who deserve representation that puts them first.

Recruitment Strategy

Building our movement requires connecting with citizens everywhere. We're leveraging social media platforms to reach millions of Americans, hosting rallies in communities across the nation, and participating in major public events where people gather. Our presence on talk radio and podcasts ensures our message reaches diverse audiences. We're looking for passionate volunteers, dedicated staff, and everyday citizens ready to take back their government. Whether you can give an hour a week or want to lead local efforts, there's a place for you in this movement.

Target Audience

Our message resonates with Americans across traditional party lines. We're reaching out to conservatives who value fiscal responsibility and individual liberty, libertarians who prioritize personal freedom and limited government, and even Green Party supporters who share our commitment to practical environmental solutions. Our platform appeals to anyone tired of career politicians, government waste, and policies that benefit special interests over working families. If you believe in common-sense governance, accountability, and putting America first, you're our target audience.

We're meeting voters where they are through a comprehensive media approach. Our social media campaigns create shareable content that spreads our message organically. What We Learned

Partner Reflection - Jacob Scholfield

My Partner: Miles Bethel

Most Challenging Aspect: Creating a unified platform was challenging because we had to balance different perspectives on key issues. Finding common ground on topics like immigration and healthcare required compromise and deep discussion. We learned that even when we agreed on goals, we sometimes disagreed on the best approach to achieve them.

Role Impact: As the Manger, I focused on [describe role responsibilities]. This shaped our decisions by emphasizing [what your role prioritized]. My partner's role as [their role] balanced my perspective by focusing on [their priorities].

Key Lesson: This project taught me that political parties must constantly negotiate between different viewpoints within their coalition. Success requires clear communication, willingness to compromise, and a shared vision that unites diverse perspectives toward common goals.

Partner Reflection - Miles Bethel

My Partner: Jacob Schofield

Most Challenging Aspect: The most difficult part about creating a unified platform was the overall minutia. There are so many laws already in place that I feel complicates the system beyond human awareness. By human awareness, I mean it is simply impossible to be aware of all federal laws and how to simultaneously obey them. Deciding which issues to prioritize and how to clearly articulate positions that would appeal to our target audience while staying true to our principles was difficult and time-consuming. We found that it was virtually impossible for us to define what we would like to change without under-defining something else entirely, or violating some other statute. We spent a majority considerable time debating the nuances of each platform plank.

Role Impact: As a Candidate, the decisions I made were based around public opinion and public image, rather than how many laws we would need to change to implement our party. In essence, the decisions were made based on how we felt about them in relation to how the public would react.

Key Lesson: I learned that political parties function as coalitions that must appeal to multiple constituencies while maintaining a coherent identity. I learned there is a reason why campaigns are costly and require no small effort or funds.
